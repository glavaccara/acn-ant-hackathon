# Cash Compass — 5-Minute Pitch

> **The agentic budget coach you can actually ship.**

---

## Opening

Thank you for the time. I'm here to talk to you about **Cash Compass** — the **agentic budget coach you can actually ship**. I have five minutes, so let me get straight to it.

---

## 1. The Problem

Let me start with the problem you're already living with.

Your customers want a **coach**. You're shipping a **dashboard**.

**Revolut, N26**, and a dozen other fintechs are winning the next generation of customers by making their app **actually do things**. Your personal-finance widgets show **pie charts** — and the customer still does all the work. **Categorising. Budgeting. Deciding.**

You already hold **the data, the rate sheet, and the write APIs**. Today, **nothing connects them** into a customer outcome.

---

## 2. The Solution

So that's the gap. Here's how we close it.

**Cash Compass embeds in your app — white-label, in your tenancy** — and it does the work for the customer. **Three things**, very simply:

- **First, it cleans up their money**: categorising every transaction, adjusting monthly budgets as habits change, and quietly surfacing the **subscription nobody's used in months**.
- **Second, it answers the big questions** — *can I afford a three-hundred-and-fifty-thousand-euro mortgage? What if rates go up two hundred basis points?* — **stress-tested in seconds, with every figure traceable to its source**.
- **And third, it acts only when allowed.** Anything that moves real money **asks for confirmation first**. Anything risky is **blocked before the agent can even try**.

---

## 3. Meet Marco — A Day in the Life

Now, that's the abstract. Let me make it concrete.

Meet **Marco** — thirty-four years old, your customer.

- **Monday morning**, his rent posts. **Already classified**, his housing budget already adjusted. **No notification, no friction** — the boring stuff just happens.
- **At lunch**, an ambiguous card payment shows up. *Was that groceries, or a restaurant?* Cash Compass **asks once, learns the merchant, never asks again**.
- **Wednesday afternoon** — and this is where **the bank becomes the hero** — Marco gets a quiet nudge: he's been paying for a streaming service every month for over a year. *Still want it?* **One tap to pause.** He **saves over a hundred euros a year**. And critically, **the agent asks before it acts**. **No surprise charges, ever.**
- **Saturday night**, the big question every bank wants to be there for. *Can I afford that flat in Navigli?* **Six seconds later**: a **stress-tested affordability report**. **Every number cited. Disclaimer included.** Marco walks into his broker meeting on Monday **already informed**.

---

## 4. What It Means for You — Four P&L Levers

That's the customer side. Now let me flip it around — what does it mean for you? **Four levers on the profit and loss.**

- **Retention goes up** — a coach customers open every day is a **primary-account feature**, and **switching costs go up** with it.
- **Net Promoter Score goes up** — the bank surfaces the **saved subscription**, the **over-budget envelope**, the **better mortgage moment**. **Customers feel served.**
- **Cross-sell goes up** — the agent already knows when a **savings goal becomes realistic**, or when a **mortgage conversation makes sense**.
- **Cost-to-serve goes down** — the long tail of **contact-centre questions** starts getting answered **inside the app**.

The pilot scorecard measures **all four**. Against **your own customer cohort**. **Your data, your numbers.**

---

## 5. Risk — The Boundary First

Of course, none of this matters if your **risk team won't let it ship**.

So we built **the boundary first, and the agent inside it**.

Most so-called AI agents rely on **telling the model not to do things**. **Ours simply cannot do them.**

- **Dangerous actions don't exist as options.**
- **Risky ones are gated by code your team controls** — not by a prompt the model can talk its way around.
- **Every number the customer sees is auditable back to its source.**
- **Hallucinated figures are mechanically rejected** before they ever reach the screen.

**Compliance signs off once. The boundary holds forever.**

Which raises a fair question. **Why Claude? Why this stack?** Three reasons.

2. **Enterprise-ready on day one.** Available through **AWS Bedrock**, in **European regions**. Your customer data **stays in your AWS tenancy**. **Procurement and data-protection conversations are weeks shorter.**

## 7. Trade-offs — In the Spirit of Trust

Now, in the spirit of building trust, let me also tell you **where this stack costs us**. **Three trade-offs** to know before you sign.

1. **Per-call cost is real.** A premium model isn't free. We mitigate by using a **smaller, cheaper model for about eighty percent of the work**. **Only the orchestrator pays premium pricing.**
2. **Latency is a constraint.** Heavy answers take **three to six seconds**. **Fine for advise-me moments, too slow for transaction-time interfaces.** We design around it.
3. **One vendor, one model family.** If Anthropic has a bad day, we have a bad day. **Bedrock gives us AWS-side resilience**, and **the math behind affordability still runs even if the model is offline**.

I'm telling you these because **your risk team will ask anyway**. **Better the answers come from us first.**
